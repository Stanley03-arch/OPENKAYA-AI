**Luo Conversations and Dialogues (Wuoyo mag Dholuo)**

**Conversation 1: Meeting Someone New**
A: **Misawa!**
B: **Misawa ahinya! Idhi nade?**
A: **Adhi maber. In idhi nade?**
B: **An bende adhi maber. In ng'a?**
A: **An en Otieno. In ng'a?**
B: **An en Akinyi. Mor romo kodi.**
A: **An bende amor romo kodi.**

Translation:
A: Hello!
B: Hello! How are you?
A: I am fine. How are you?
B: I am also fine. Who are you?
A: I am Otieno. Who are you?
B: I am Akinyi. I'm happy to meet you.
A: I'm also happy to meet you.

**Conversation 2: At the Market**
A: **Samaki adi?**
B: **Rech adi? Wan nigo rech mathoth.**
A: **Rech achiel en pesa adi?**
B: **Rech achiel en shilingi mia achiel.**
A: **Ber. Amiya rech ariyo.**
B: **Erokamano. En shilingi mia ariyo.**
A: **Erokamano ahinya.**

Translation:
A: Which fish?
B: Which fish? We have many fish.
A: How much is one fish?
B: One fish is one hundred shillings.
A: OK. Give me two fish.
B: Thank you. It's two hundred shillings.
A: Thank you very much.

**Conversation 3: Asking for Directions**
A: **Kiyie, skul ni kanye?**
B: **Skul ni machiegni. Dhi tir yo ma korachwich.**
A: **Bor adi?**
B: **Ok bor. Wuoth dakika abich kende.**
A: **Erokamano ahinya.**
B: **Onge tim. Dhi maber.**

Translation:
A: Excuse me, where is the school?
B: The school is nearby. Go straight on the left road.
A: How far?
B: Not far. Walk only five minutes.
A: Thank you very much.
B: You're welcome. Go well.

**Conversation 4: At Home**
Wuoro: **Chiemo oikre?**
Min: **Ee, chiemo oikre. Luong nyithindo mondo gibi chiemo.**
Wuoro: **Otieno! Akinyi! Biuru chiemo!**
Otieno: **Wabiro kawuono. Chiemo en ang'o?**
Min: **Kuon gi rech.**
Akinyi: **Mamit ahinya! An ahero kuon gi rech.**

Translation:
Father: Is food ready?
Mother: Yes, food is ready. Call the children to come eat.
Father: Otieno! Akinyi! Come eat!
Otieno: We're coming now. What is the food?
Mother: Ugali and fish.
Akinyi: Very delicious! I love ugali and fish.

**Conversation 5: Planning to Go Somewhere**
A: **Kiny idhi kanye?**
B: **Kiny adhi Kisumu. In bende ibiro dhi?**
A: **Ee, adwaro dhi. Wabiro dhi sa adi?**
B: **Wabiro dhi okinyi sa abiriyo.**
A: **Ber ahinya. Wabiro dhi gi ang'o?**
B: **Wabiro dhi gi matatu.**
A: **Maber. Aneno kiny.**

Translation:
A: Where are you going tomorrow?
B: Tomorrow I'm going to Kisumu. Are you also going?
A: Yes, I want to go. What time will we go?
B: We will go in the morning at 8 o'clock.
A: Very good. How will we go?
B: We will go by matatu (minibus).
A: OK. See you tomorrow.

**Conversation 6: Talking About Weather**
A: **Kawuono chwech nade?**
B: **Kawuono chieng' rieny ahinya.**
A: **Adier. Liet ahinya.**
B: **Iparo ni koth biro chue?**
A: **Ee, aneno bor polo. Koth biro chue odhiambo.**
B: **Ber. Chieng' koro liet mokalo.**

Translation:
A: How is the weather today?
B: Today the sun is very bright.
A: True. It's very hot.
B: Do you think it will rain?
A: Yes, I see clouds. It will rain in the evening.
B: Good. The sun is now too hot.

**Conversation 7: At School**
Japuonj: **Oyaore nyithindo!**
Nyithindo: **Oyaore japuonj!**
Japuonj: **Kawuono wabiro somo Dholuo. Un ng'eyo Dholuo?**
Jasomo: **Ee, wang'eyo Dholuo matin.**
Japuonj: **Ber. Wach "erokamano" e Dholuo.**
Jasomo: **Erokamano.**
Japuonj: **Maber ahinya! Un osomo maber.**

Translation:
Teacher: Good morning children!
Children: Good morning teacher!
Teacher: Today we will study Luo. Do you know Luo?
Student: Yes, we know a little Luo.
Teacher: Good. Say "thank you" in Luo.
Student: Erokamano.
Teacher: Very good! You have studied well.

**Conversation 8: Talking About Family**
A: **In nigo owete adi?**
B: **An nigo owete adek. Ariyo gin yawuowi to achiel en nyako.**
A: **Gin dak kanye?**
B: **Gin dak Nairobi. In nigo owete?**
A: **An nigo owadwa achiel kende. En dak dala gi wuora gi minwa.**
B: **Dalau ni kanye?**
A: **Dalawa ni Siaya, machiegni gi Kisumu.**

Translation:
A: How many siblings do you have?
B: I have three siblings. Two are boys and one is a girl.
A: Where do they live?
B: They live in Nairobi. Do you have siblings?
A: I have only one sibling. He lives at home with our father and mother.
B: Where is your home?
A: Our home is in Siaya, near Kisumu.

**Conversation 9: Expressing Feelings**
A: **Idhi nade kawuono?**
B: **Ok adhi maber. Amor.**
A: **Ang'o omiyo imor?**
B: **Amor nikech ne ok aloso exam maber.**
A: **Kik iparri. Exam machielo ibiro timo maber.**
B: **Erokamano kuom jiwo chunya.**
A: **Onge tim. Wan osiepewa.**

Translation:
A: How are you today?
B: I'm not well. I'm sad.
A: Why are you sad?
B: I'm sad because I didn't pass the exam well.
A: Don't worry. The next exam you will do well.
B: Thank you for encouraging me.
A: You're welcome. We are friends.

**Conversation 10: Making Plans**
A: **Jumapil ibiro timo ang'o?**
B: **Jumapil abiro dhi kanisa. Bang'e abiro dhi limo osiepena.**
A: **Maber. An abiro dhi Kit Mikayi gi jooda.**
B: **Kit Mikayi ber ahinya! Ubiro kawo foto mangʼeny?**
A: **Ee, wabiro kawo foto mathoth. In bende inyalo biro?**
B: **Ooyo, ok anyal. To oruru maber.**
A: **Erokamano. Wan bende wabiro orore maber.**

Translation:
A: What will you do on Sunday?
B: On Sunday I will go to church. After that I will go visit my friend.
A: OK. I will go to Kit Mikayi with my family.
B: Kit Mikayi is very beautiful! Will you take many photos?
A: Yes, we will take many photos. Can you also come?
B: No, I cannot. But enjoy yourselves.
A: Thank you. We will also enjoy ourselves.
