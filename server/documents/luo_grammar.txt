**Luo Grammar and Sentence Structures (Chik mag Dholuo)**

**Basic Sentence Structure:**
Luo typically follows Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) word order, similar to English.

**Personal Pronouns (Nying Ngato):**
- **An** = I/Me
- **In** = You (singular)
- **En** = He/She/It
- **Wan** = We/Us
- **Un** = You (plural)
- **Gin** = They/Them

**Possessive Pronouns:**
- **Mara** = My/Mine
- **Mari** = Your/Yours (singular)
- **Mare** = His/Her/Its
- **Marwa** = Our/Ours
- **Maru** = Your/Yours (plural)
- **Margi** = Their/Theirs

**Present Tense (Kinde ma Kawuono):**
To form present tense, use the pronoun + verb:
- **An adhi** = I go
- **In idhi** = You go
- **En odhi** = He/She goes
- **Wan wadhi** = We go
- **Un udhi** = You (plural) go
- **Gin gidhi** = They go

**Past Tense (Kinde Mosekalo):**
Past tense is formed by adding "ne" before the verb:
- **An ne adhi** = I went
- **In ne idhi** = You went
- **En ne odhi** = He/She went
- **Wan ne wadhi** = We went
- **Un ne udhi** = You (plural) went
- **Gin ne gidhi** = They went

**Future Tense (Kinde Mabiro):**
Future tense uses "biro" (will) before the verb:
- **An biro dhi** = I will go
- **In biro dhi** = You will go
- **En biro dhi** = He/She will go
- **Wan biro dhi** = We will go
- **Un biro dhi** = You (plural) will go
- **Gin biro dhi** = They will go

**Negative Sentences (Weche Makwero):**
Negation is formed using "ok" (not):
- **An ok adhi** = I don't go / I'm not going
- **In ok idhi** = You don't go
- **En ok odhi** = He/She doesn't go
- **An ne ok adhi** = I didn't go
- **An ok biro dhi** = I will not go

**Question Formation (Goyo Penjo):**
Questions can be formed by:
1. Using question words at the beginning:
   - **Ng'a odhi?** = Who went?
   - **Ang'o itimo?** = What are you doing?
   - **Kanye idhi?** = Where are you going?

2. Raising intonation at the end of a statement:
   - **Idhi skul?** = Are you going to school?
   - **In ichiemo?** = Are you eating?

**Common Sentence Patterns:**

**"To be" sentences:**
- **An en japuonj** = I am a teacher
- **In en ng'a?** = Who are you?
- **En en dala** = He/She is at home

**"To have" sentences (using "ni" or "nigo"):**
- **An nigo buku** = I have a book
- **En nigo dhiang'** = He/She has a cow
- **Wan nigo gari** = We have a car

**Location sentences (using "ni"):**
- **An ni dala** = I am at home
- **Buku ni e mesa** = The book is on the table
- **Kisumu ni e Nam Lolwe** = Kisumu is at Lake Victoria

**Desire/Want (using "dwaro"):**
- **An adwaro chiemo** = I want food
- **En odwaro dhi** = He/She wants to go
- **Wan wadwaro pi** = We want water

**Ability/Can (using "nyalo"):**
- **An anyalo wuoyo Dholuo** = I can speak Luo
- **In inyalo konyo?** = Can you help?
- **En ok onyalo biro** = He/She cannot come

**Comparison:**
- **Maduong' moloyo** = Bigger than
- **Maber moloyo** = Better than
- **En duong' moloyo an** = He/She is bigger than me

**Conjunctions (Weche Mariwo):**
- **Gi** = And/With
- **Kata** = Or
- **To** = But
- **Nikech** = Because
- **Ka** = If/When
- **Kapok** = Before
- **Bang'e** = After

**Example Sentences:**

**Simple Sentences:**
1. **An adhi skul** = I go to school
2. **En ochiemo kuon** = He/She is eating ugali
3. **Wan waneno chieng'** = We see the sun
4. **Gin gidhi ohala** = They are going to the market
5. **Nyathi nindo** = The child is sleeping

**Complex Sentences:**
1. **An adhi skul nikech adwaro somo** = I go to school because I want to study
2. **Ka koth ochue, ok wabiro dhi** = If it rains, we will not go
3. **En ochiemo kuon gi rech** = He/She is eating ugali and fish
4. **An ahero Dholuo to ok anyalo wuoyo maber** = I love Luo but I cannot speak well
5. **Wan ne wadhi dala nyoro** = We went home yesterday

**Polite Requests:**
1. **Kiyie ikonya?** = Please, can you help me?
2. **Kiyie imiya pi** = Please give me water
3. **Kiyie iluonga** = Please call me

**Commands (Chike):**
1. **Dhi!** = Go!
2. **Bi!** = Come!
3. **Chiemo!** = Eat!
4. **Bed maber!** = Stay well! / Be good!
5. **Kik itim kamano!** = Don't do that!
