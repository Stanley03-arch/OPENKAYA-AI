**Luo Idioms and Expressions (Ngeche gi Weche Mopondo)**

**Common Idioms:**

1. **Goyo wang' piny** (Literally: "To hit the eye of the ground")
   - Meaning: To be embarrassed or ashamed
   - Usage: Ne ogoyo wang' piny kane owuok e exam. (He was embarrassed when he failed the exam.)

2. **Mako wang'** (Literally: "To catch the eye")
   - Meaning: To attract attention, to be impressive
   - Usage: Ote manyien mare omako wang' ji duto. (His new house caught everyone's attention.)

3. **Tho e chuny** (Literally: "To die in the heart")
   - Meaning: To be deeply disappointed or heartbroken
   - Usage: Ne atho e chuny kane ne ok ayier. (I was heartbroken when I wasn't selected.)

4. **Chiemo wang'** (Literally: "To eat the eye")
   - Meaning: To be jealous or envious
   - Usage: Osiepene chiemo wangʼe nikech loch manyien. (His friend is jealous because of the new job.)

5. **Goyo it piny** (Literally: "To put the ear down")
   - Meaning: To listen carefully, to pay attention
   - Usage: Goyo it piny mondo iwinj wach maduong'. (Listen carefully to hear important news.)

6. **Loko wang'** (Literally: "To turn the eye")
   - Meaning: To ignore someone deliberately
   - Usage: Ok onego ilok wang' ne joma dwaro konyi. (You shouldn't ignore people who want to help you.)

7. **Bedo gi chuny ariyo** (Literally: "To have two hearts")
   - Meaning: To be indecisive or double-minded
   - Usage: Kik ibed gi chuny ariyo, yier gimoro achiel. (Don't be indecisive, choose one thing.)

8. **Ng'ado wang'** (Literally: "To cut the eye")
   - Meaning: To make eye contact, to look directly at someone
   - Usage: Ne ok onyal ng'ado wang' gi jaduong'. (He couldn't make eye contact with the elder.)

9. **Chiemo pesa** (Literally: "To eat money")
   - Meaning: To embezzle or misuse funds
   - Usage: Jatelo ne ochiemo pesa mar oganda. (The leader embezzled community funds.)

10. **Goyo wich tek** (Literally: "To hit the head hard")
    - Meaning: To think deeply, to concentrate hard
    - Usage: Goyo wich tek mondo iyud dwoko. (Think hard to find the answer.)

11. **Bedo gi it marabich** (Literally: "To have five ears")
    - Meaning: To be very attentive or alert
    - Usage: Jarit nyaka bed gi it marabich. (A guard must be very alert.)

12. **Loko dhok** (Literally: "To change the mouth/language")
    - Meaning: To change one's story or contradict oneself
    - Usage: Ok ber loko dhok pile ka pile. (It's not good to keep changing your story.)

13. **Mako tiend** (Literally: "To catch the foot")
    - Meaning: To find fault with someone, to catch someone in a mistake
    - Usage: Gin dwaro mana mako tienda. (They just want to catch me in a mistake.)

14. **Chwero bad** (Literally: "To shake hands")
    - Meaning: To make peace, to reconcile
    - Usage: Bang lweny, ne gichwero bad. (After the fight, they made peace.)

15. **Goyo wang' wangʼ** (Literally: "To hit eye to eye")
    - Meaning: To confront directly, face to face
    - Usage: Wabiro goyo wang' wang' gi jatelo. (We will confront the leader directly.)

**Expressions About Character:**

16. **Ng'at ma nigi chuny maduong'** (Person with a big heart)
    - Meaning: A generous or kind person
    - Usage: En ng'at ma nigi chuny maduong', konyogi duto. (He's a generous person, he helps everyone.)

17. **Ng'at ma nigi chuny matek** (Person with a hard heart)
    - Meaning: A stubborn or cruel person
    - Usage: Ok onyal loko pache, en gi chuny matek. (He can't change his mind, he's stubborn.)

18. **Ng'at ma nigi wang' mabor** (Person with far eyes)
    - Meaning: A visionary person, someone who plans ahead
    - Usage: Jaduong'wa nigi wang' mabor, oneno gik mabiro. (Our elder is visionary, he sees what's coming.)

19. **Ng'at ma nigi dhok maduong'** (Person with a big mouth)
    - Meaning: A talkative person or someone who can't keep secrets
    - Usage: Kik inyise wach mopondo, en gi dhok maduong'. (Don't tell him secrets, he has a big mouth.)

20. **Ng'at ma nigi lwedo malo** (Person with high support)
    - Meaning: Someone who is well-connected or influential
    - Usage: En nigi lwedo malo e serikal. (He has high connections in government.)

**Expressions About Life:**

21. **Ng'ima ok nyal rito ng'ato** (Life doesn't wait for anyone)
    - Meaning: Life goes on regardless of circumstances
    - Usage: Ng'ima ok nyal rito ng'ato, nyaka ibed motegno. (Life doesn't wait, you must be ready.)

22. **Chieng' ka chieng'** (Day by day)
    - Meaning: Taking things one day at a time
    - Usage: Wadak chieng' ka chieng', ok wang'eyo kiny. (We live day by day, we don't know tomorrow.)

23. **Kinde duto nigi thuolone** (Every time has its snake)
    - Meaning: Every era has its challenges
    - Usage: Kinde duto nigi thuolone, to nyaka wabed motegno. (Every time has challenges, but we must be strong.)

24. **Piny opong' gi weche** (The world is full of things)
    - Meaning: There are many things happening, life is complex
    - Usage: Piny opong' gi weche, ok inyal ng'eyo duto. (The world is full of things, you can't know everything.)

**Expressions About Relationships:**

25. **Osiep adier** (True friend)
    - Meaning: A genuine, loyal friend
    - Usage: Osiep adier nyiso kore e kinde mar chandruok. (A true friend shows themselves in times of trouble.)

26. **Osiep ma chiemo kodi** (Friend who eats with you)
    - Meaning: A fair-weather friend (often used negatively)
    - Usage: En mana osiep ma chiemo kodi, ok okonyi ka in gi chandruok. (He's just a fair-weather friend, he won't help when you're in trouble.)

27. **Wuon dala** (Owner of the home)
    - Meaning: The head of household, the responsible one
    - Usage: Kaka wuon dala, nyaka arit jooda. (As head of household, I must take care of my family.)

28. **Nyako ma ber** (A good girl/woman)
    - Meaning: A well-behaved, virtuous woman
    - Usage: Nyako ma ber ng'ere gi timbe maber. (A good woman is known by good behavior.)

**Expressions About Work and Success:**

29. **Tich gi loch** (Work and wealth)
    - Meaning: Hard work leads to prosperity
    - Usage: Tich gi loch wuotho kaachiel. (Work and wealth go together.)

30. **Ng'at ma tiyo gi lwete** (Person who works with their hands)
    - Meaning: A hardworking, practical person
    - Usage: En ng'at ma tiyo gi lwete, ok odew tich. (He's a hardworking person, he doesn't fear work.)

31. **Loso nyinge** (To build one's name)
    - Meaning: To establish a good reputation
    - Usage: Nyaka itim tich maber mondo ilos nyingi. (You must do good work to build your reputation.)

32. **Goyo tich piny** (To put work down)
    - Meaning: To complete a task successfully
    - Usage: Ne ogoyo tich piny maber ahinya. (He completed the task very well.)

**Expressions About Wisdom:**

33. **Winjo gi it ariyo** (To hear with two ears)
    - Meaning: To listen to both sides before judging
    - Usage: Jangʼad bura nyaka winj gi it ariyo. (A judge must hear both sides.)

34. **Ng'eyo gi rieko** (To know with wisdom)
    - Meaning: To understand deeply, with insight
    - Usage: Jodongo ng'eyo gi rieko. (Elders understand with wisdom.)

35. **Wach moro ok yal wach moro** (One word is not like another)
    - Meaning: Words have different weights and meanings
    - Usage: Wach moro ok yal wach moro, par kapok iwuoyo. (Words differ, think before you speak.)

**Expressions About Food and Hospitality:**

36. **Chiemo gi ji** (To eat with people)
    - Meaning: To share meals, to be hospitable
    - Usage: Jaluo hera chiemo gi ji. (Luo people love eating with others.)

37. **Ot ma ok nigi wendo** (A house without visitors)
    - Meaning: A lonely or unwelcoming place
    - Usage: Ot ma ok nigi wendo chalo gi bur. (A house without visitors is like a grave.)

38. **Pog chiemo** (To share food)
    - Meaning: To be generous and hospitable
    - Usage: Pog chiemo gi jowadu en tim maber. (Sharing food with neighbors is good.)

**Modern Expressions:**

39. **Bedo e simu** (To be on the phone)
    - Meaning: To be constantly on the phone
    - Usage: Nyithindo kawuono bedo e simu pile. (Children today are always on the phone.)

40. **Neno e TV** (To see on TV)
    - Meaning: To watch television
    - Usage: Ne aneno wach e TV. (I saw the news on TV.)

41. **Dhi online** (To go online)
    - Meaning: To use the internet
    - Usage: Adhi online mondo anon wach. (I go online to check news.)

42. **Mako foto** (To take a photo)
    - Meaning: To take a photograph
    - Usage: Wakmako foto ka wan Kit Mikayi. (We took photos when we were at Kit Mikayi.)

**Expressions About Time:**

43. **Sa ma ok onego** (Time that shouldn't)
    - Meaning: The wrong time, inappropriate timing
    - Usage: Nobiro e sa ma ok onego. (He came at the wrong time.)

44. **Kinde osechopo** (Time has arrived)
    - Meaning: It's time, the moment has come
    - Usage: Kinde osechopo mar dhi skul. (It's time to go to school.)

45. **Kinde ok rit ng'ato** (Time doesn't wait for anyone)
    - Meaning: Time waits for no one
    - Usage: Kinde ok rit ng'ato, nyaka ibed motegno. (Time doesn't wait, you must be ready.)

**Expressions About Respect:**

46. **Miyo luor** (To give respect)
    - Meaning: To show respect
    - Usage: Nyaka imiyo jodongo luor. (You must show elders respect.)

47. **Ng'at ma ok nigi luor** (Person without respect)
    - Meaning: A disrespectful person
    - Usage: Ng'at ma ok nigi luor ok nyal dongo maber. (A disrespectful person cannot grow well.)

**Expressions About Money:**

48. **Pesa ok dongo e yath** (Money doesn't grow on trees)
    - Meaning: Money must be earned through work
    - Usage: Pesa ok dongo e yath, nyaka iti. (Money doesn't grow on trees, you must work.)

49. **Ng'at ma nigi pesa** (Person who has money)
    - Meaning: A wealthy person
    - Usage: Ng'at ma nigi pesa nyaka rit maber. (A wealthy person must be careful.)

50. **Loso mwandu** (To build wealth)
    - Meaning: To accumulate wealth or property
    - Usage: Oloso mwandu gi tich matek. (He built wealth through hard work.)
