**Complete Luo Phrase Book (Buk Mar Weche Dholuo)**

**SECTION 1: ESSENTIAL PHRASES**

**Greetings & Farewells:**
- Nango? = How are you?
- Adhi maber = I'm fine
- In nango? = And you?
- Oyawore = Good morning
- Oimore = Good afternoon
- Otieno = Good evening
- Inindo nade? = Did you sleep well?
- Nindo maber = Good night
- Oriti = Goodbye (to one staying)
- Odhi ber = Goodbye (to one leaving)
- Wanere kiny = See you tomorrow
- Wanere bang'e = See you later

**Polite Expressions:**
- Erokamano = Thank you
- Erokamano ahinya = Thank you very much
- Kiyie = Please
- Mos = Sorry/Excuse me
- Mos ahinya = Very sorry
- Onge gima = You're welcome / It's nothing
- Ee = Yes
- Ooyo = No
- Adier = True/Really
- Kamano = Like that/So
- Maber = Good/OK
- Kare = Alright

**SECTION 2: INTRODUCTIONS**

**Meeting People:**
- Nyingi ng'a? = What is your name?
- Nyinga en... = My name is...
- Mor romo kodi = Nice to meet you
- Ia kanye? = Where are you from?
- Aa... = I'm from...
- Idak kanye? = Where do you live?
- Adak... = I live in...
- In gi higni adi? = How old are you?
- An gi higni... = I am... years old
- Itimo tich mane? = What work do you do?
- An ja... = I am a...
- Isomo kanye? = Where do you study?
- Asomo... = I study at...

**Family:**
- In gi joodi adi? = How many family members do you have?
- An gi jooda... = I have... family members
- Isekendo? = Are you married?
- Ee, asekendo = Yes, I'm married
- Ooyo, pok akendo = No, I'm not married yet
- In gi nyithindo adi? = How many children do you have?
- An gi nyithindo... = I have... children
- Wuonu nying'i ng'a? = What is your father's name?
- Minu nying'i ng'a? = What is your mother's name?

**SECTION 3: DAILY CONVERSATION**

**Asking Questions:**
- Ang'o? = What?
- Ng'a? = Who?
- Kanye? = Where?
- Sa adi? = What time? / When?
- Kare ang'o? = Why?
- Nade? = How?
- Adi? = How many/much?
- Mane? = Which one?
- Itimo ang'o? = What are you doing?
- Idhi kanye? = Where are you going?
- Ibiro sa adi? = What time are you coming?

**Expressing Needs:**
- Adwaro... = I want...
- Anyalo yudo...? = Can I get...?
- An gi kech = I'm hungry
- An gi riyo = I'm thirsty
- Atuo = I'm sick
- Aol = I'm tired
- An gi luoro = I'm afraid
- Adagi = I'm lost
- Akony = I need help
- Anyalo... = I can...
- Ok anyal... = I cannot...

**SECTION 4: TIME & DATES**

**Time:**
- Sa adi sani? = What time is it now?
- En sa... = It's... o'clock
- Sa achiel = 1 o'clock
- Sa ariyo = 2 o'clock
- Sa abich = 5 o'clock
- Okinyi = Morning
- Odiechieng' = Afternoon
- Odhiambo = Evening
- Otieno = Night
- Dakika apar = 10 minutes
- Sa achiel gi nus = 1:30 (one and half)

**Days:**
- Kawuono = Today
- Nyoro = Yesterday
- Kiny = Tomorrow
- Tich Ariyo = Monday
- Tich Adek = Tuesday
- Tich Ang'wen = Wednesday
- Tich Abich = Thursday
- Tich Abich Gariyo = Friday
- Jumamosi/Ngeso = Saturday
- Jumapili/Pil = Sunday

**Months:**
- Dwe mar achiel = January
- Dwe mar ariyo = February
- Dwe mar adek = March
- Dwe mar ang'wen = April
- Dwe mar abich = May
- Dwe mar auchiel = June
- Dwe mar abiriyo = July
- Dwe mar aboro = August
- Dwe mar ochiko = September
- Dwe mar apar = October
- Dwe mar apar gachiel = November
- Dwe mar apar gariyo = December

**SECTION 5: SHOPPING & MONEY**

**At the Market:**
- Gimani en pesa adi? = How much is this?
- En shilingi... = It's... shillings
- Tek ahinya = Too expensive
- Inyalo dwoko pesa? = Can you reduce the price?
- Miyo loko = Give change
- Ng'iewo gimani adi? = How many of these will you buy?
- Abiro ng'iewo... = I will buy...
- In gi...? = Do you have...?
- Onge = There isn't any / We don't have
- Nitie = There is / We have
- Achul nade? = How do I pay?
- Chul gi pesa = Pay with cash
- Chul gi M-Pesa = Pay with M-Pesa

**SECTION 6: FOOD & DINING**

**Ordering Food:**
- Adwaro chiemo = I want food
- Adwaro metho = I want to drink
- Chiemo en ang'o? = What food is there?
- Wan gi... = We have...
- Miyo... = Give me...
- Kelona... = Bring me...
- Kuon gi rech = Ugali and fish
- Kuon gi alode = Ugali and vegetables
- Chai = Tea
- Kahawa = Coffee
- Pi = Water
- Soda = Soda
- Chiemo ber = The food is good
- Chiemo mamit = Delicious food
- Achul pesa adi? = How much do I pay?

**SECTION 7: DIRECTIONS**

**Finding Places:**
- ... ni kanye? = Where is...?
- Kaka adhi...? = How do I get to...?
- Bor adi? = How far is it?
- Ok bor = Not far
- Bor matin = A little far
- Bor ahinya = Very far
- Dhi tir = Go straight
- Lok yo korachwich = Turn right
- Lok yo koracham = Turn left
- Ng'ad yo = Cross the road
- Chung' ka = Stop here
- Dhi nyime = Continue/Go forward
- Dok chien = Go back

**SECTION 8: TRANSPORTATION**

**Taking Transport:**
- Matatu madhi... ni kanye? = Where is the matatu to...?
- Bas madhi... = Bus going to...
- Pesa adi nyaka...? = How much to...?
- Achul pesa adi? = How much do I pay?
- Lor en pesa adi? = How much is the fare?
- Chung'a... = Stop at...
- Asechopo = I've arrived
- Gol ka = Let me out here
- Wuoth kawo kinde adi? = How long does the journey take?

**SECTION 9: ACCOMMODATION**

**At a Hotel:**
- In gi ot? = Do you have a room?
- Ot en pesa adi e otieno? = How much is a room per night?
- Adwaro ot mar ng'ato achiel = I want a single room
- Adwaro ot mar ji ariyo = I want a double room
- Ot nigi ang'o? = What does the room have?
- Nigi bathroom? = Does it have a bathroom?
- Nigi Wi-Fi? = Does it have Wi-Fi?
- Abiro bedo ndalo adi? = How many days will I stay?
- Abiro bedo ndalo... = I will stay... days

**SECTION 10: HEALTH**

**At the Hospital:**
- Atuo = I'm sick
- Denda ok ber = My health is not good
- Rem mara ni kanye? = Where is my pain?
- Rem mara ni e... = My pain is in...
- Rem mar wich = Headache
- Rem mar ich = Stomachache
- Rem mar lak = Toothache
- An gi liet = I have fever
- Akoko = I'm coughing
- Angilo = I'm vomiting
- An gi diep = I have diarrhea
- Adwaro jadak = I want a doctor
- Adwaro yath = I want medicine
- Anyalo yudo yath kanye? = Where can I get medicine?

**SECTION 11: EMERGENCIES**

**Getting Help:**
- Kony! = Help!
- Chandruok! = Problem/Trouble!
- Luong' polisi! = Call the police!
- Luong' jadak! = Call a doctor!
- An gi chandruok = I have a problem
- Gimora okwal = Someone stole my thing
- Asedagi = I'm lost
- Adwaro dhi... = I want to go to...
- Inyalo konya? = Can you help me?
- Anyalo goyo simu? = Can I make a call?
- Luong' ambulance = Call an ambulance

**SECTION 12: WEATHER**

**Talking About Weather:**
- Piny chalo nade? = How is the weather?
- Piny ber = The weather is good
- Piny rach = The weather is bad
- Piny liet = It's hot
- Piny ngʼich = It's cold
- Koth chue = It's raining
- Koth biro chue = It will rain
- Chieng' rieny = The sun is shining
- Yamo tugo = The wind is blowing
- Polo oimre = It's cloudy

**SECTION 13: TECHNOLOGY**

**Using Technology:**
- In gi simu? = Do you have a phone?
- Namba mari en adi? = What is your number?
- Inyalo miya namba mari? = Can you give me your number?
- In gi intanet? = Do you have internet?
- Wi-Fi password en ang'o? = What is the Wi-Fi password?
- Simu mara orumo betri = My phone battery is dead
- Anyalo moko simu ka? = Can I charge my phone here?
- Inyalo kelona charger? = Can you bring me a charger?
- Kaka atim download? = How do I download?
- Oro ujumbe = Send a message

**SECTION 14: WORK & BUSINESS**

**At Work:**
- Tich dhi nade? = How is work going?
- Tich dhi maber = Work is going well
- Achako tich sa adi? = What time do I start work?
- Agik tich sa adi? = What time do I finish work?
- An gi meeting sa adi? = What time is my meeting?
- Inyalo konya gi...? = Can you help me with...?
- Nyaka atim ang'o? = What should I do?
- Wach mani en maduong' = This matter is important
- Nyaka watim kamano = We must do it that way
- Ayie kod wachni = I agree with this
- Ok ayie = I don't agree
