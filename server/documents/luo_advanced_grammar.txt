**Advanced Luo Grammar (Chik Mag Dholuo Matut)**

**Advanced Tense Formations:**

**1. Present Continuous (Kinde ma Kawuono Mamedo):**
Used to describe actions happening right now.

Formation: Pronoun + verb + -o/-e ending

Examples:
- **An atimo** = I am doing
- **In itimo** = You are doing  
- **En otimo** = He/She is doing
- **Wan watimo** = We are doing
- **Un utimo** = You (plural) are doing
- **Gin gitimo** = They are doing

More examples:
- **An achiemo** = I am eating
- **En owuotho** = He/She is walking
- **Gin giwer** = They are singing

**2. Present Perfect (Kinde ma Kawuono Morumo):**
Used to describe completed actions with present relevance.

Formation: Pronoun + ose + verb

Examples:
- **An osechiemo** = I have eaten
- **In osechiemo** = You have eaten
- **En osechiemo** = He/She has eaten
- **Wan wasechiemo** = We have eaten
- **Un usechiemo** = You (plural) have eaten
- **Gin gisechiemo** = They have eaten

More examples:
- **An osedhi** = I have gone
- **En osetimo** = He/She has done
- **Wan wasesomo** = We have studied

**3. Past Continuous (Kinde Mosekalo Mamedo):**
Used to describe ongoing actions in the past.

Formation: Pronoun + ne + verb + -o/-e

Examples:
- **An ne atimo** = I was doing
- **In ne itimo** = You were doing
- **En ne otimo** = He/She was doing
- **Wan ne watimo** = We were doing
- **Gin ne gitimo** = They were doing

More examples:
- **An ne achiemo** = I was eating
- **En ne owuotho** = He/She was walking
- **Gin ne giwer** = They were singing

**4. Past Perfect (Kinde Mosekalo Morumo):**
Used to describe actions completed before another past action.

Formation: Pronoun + ne + ose + verb

Examples:
- **An ne osechiemo** = I had eaten
- **In ne isechiemo** = You had eaten
- **En ne osechiemo** = He/She had eaten
- **Wan ne wasechiemo** = We had eaten
- **Gin ne gisechiemo** = They had eaten

More examples:
- **An ne osedhi** = I had gone
- **En ne osetimo** = He/She had done
- **Kane abiro, en ne osedhi** = When I came, he/she had gone

**5. Future Continuous (Kinde Mabiro Mamedo):**
Used to describe ongoing actions in the future.

Formation: Pronoun + biro + bedo + verb + -o/-e

Examples:
- **An biro bedo katimo** = I will be doing
- **In biro bedo kitimo** = You will be doing
- **En biro bedo kotimo** = He/She will be doing
- **Wan biro bedo watimo** = We will be doing
- **Gin biro bedo gitimo** = They will be doing

More examples:
- **An biro bedo kachiemo** = I will be eating
- **En biro bedo kowuotho** = He/She will be walking

**6. Future Perfect (Kinde Mabiro Morumo):**
Used to describe actions that will be completed by a certain future time.

Formation: Pronoun + biro + bedo + ose + verb

Examples:
- **An biro bedo osechiemo** = I will have eaten
- **En biro bedo osedhi** = He/She will have gone
- **Kane ibiro, an biro bedo osetimo** = When you come, I will have done it

---

**Conditional Sentences (Weche mag "Ka"):**

**1. First Conditional (Real Possibility):**
Used for real future possibilities.

Structure: Ka + present tense, future tense

Examples:
- **Ka koth ochue, ok wabiro dhi** = If it rains, we will not go
- **Ka isomo matek, ibiro loso exam** = If you study hard, you will pass the exam
- **Ka ibiro, wabiro chiemo kaachiel** = If you come, we will eat together
- **Ka in gi pesa, ng'iew gimoro** = If you have money, buy something

**2. Second Conditional (Unreal Present):**
Used for hypothetical or unlikely present situations.

Structure: Ka + past tense, would + verb

Examples:
- **Ka ne an gi pesa, da ng'iewo gari** = If I had money, I would buy a car
- **Ka ne en ni ka, da okonyo** = If he/she were here, he/she would help
- **Ka ne wang'eyo, da wawacho** = If we knew, we would tell

**3. Third Conditional (Unreal Past):**
Used for past situations that didn't happen.

Structure: Ka + past perfect, would have + verb

Examples:
- **Ka ne osechiemo, ok da otuo** = If he/she had eaten, he/she wouldn't have been sick
- **Ka ne isomo, da iloso exam** = If you had studied, you would have passed
- **Ka ne abiro mapok, da aneno** = If I had come earlier, I would have seen

---

**Subjunctive Mood (Kinde mar Dwaro):**

Used to express wishes, suggestions, or hypothetical situations.

Formation: Mondo + pronoun + verb

Examples:
- **Mondo odhi** = Let him/her go / He/she should go
- **Mondo wabi** = Let us come / We should come
- **Mondo itim** = You should do
- **Mondo gichiemo** = They should eat

More complex examples:
- **Adwaro mondo ibiro** = I want you to come
- **Oparo mondo wadhi** = He/She thinks we should go
- **Jatelo owacho mondo gitim tich** = The leader said they should work

---

**Passive Voice (Dhok ma Ok Timo Gimoro):**

Formation: Object + verb + gi/kod + subject

Examples:
- **Buku osesomo gi Otieno** = The book has been read by Otieno
- **Chiemo osetedo gi min** = Food has been cooked by mother
- **Ot oseloso gi japur** = The house has been built by the builder

Alternative passive using "ne":
- **Buku ne osomo** = The book was read
- **Chiemo ne otedo** = Food was cooked
- **Ot ne oloso** = The house was built

---

**Relative Clauses (Weche Mariwo):**

**Using "ma" (who/which/that):**

Examples:
- **Ng'at **ma** odhi** = The person who went
- **Buku **ma** asomo** = The book that I read
- **Ot **ma** en-go** = The house that he/she has
- **Chiemo **ma** min otedo** = The food that mother cooked

More complex:
- **An ahero ng'at ma nigi chuny maber** = I love a person who has a good heart
- **Buku ma asomo ne ber** = The book that I read was good
- **Ot ma ne oloso ne duong'** = The house that was built was big

---

**Reported Speech (Wach ma Osewach):**

**Direct to Indirect Speech:**

Direct: **En owacho, "Adhi skul"** = He/She said, "I am going to school"
Indirect: **En owacho ni odhi skul** = He/She said that he/she is going to school

Direct: **Omondi owacho, "Achiemo kuon"** = Omondi said, "I am eating ugali"
Indirect: **Omondi owacho ni ochiemo kuon** = Omondi said that he is eating ugali

Direct: **Ne owacho, "Abiro biro kiny"** = He/She said, "I will come tomorrow"
Indirect: **Ne owacho ni obiro biro kinyne** = He/She said that he/she would come the next day

---

**Causative Verbs (Weche ma Miyo Gimoro Timore):**

Formation: Add suffix to make someone do something

Examples:
- **Chiemo** (to eat) → **Chiembo** (to feed)
- **Nindo** (to sleep) → **Nindbo** (to put to sleep)
- **Dhi** (to go) → **Ter** (to send/make go)
- **Somo** (to read) → **Sombo** (to teach to read)

Usage:
- **Min chiembo nyathi** = Mother is feeding the child
- **Wuoro tero Otieno skul** = Father is sending Otieno to school
- **Japuonj sombo nyithindo** = The teacher is teaching children to read

---

**Reciprocal Actions (Weche ma Ji Timo Kaachiel):**

Formation: Use "gi" or add reciprocal suffix

Examples:
- **Goyo** (to hit) → **Goyore** (to hit each other)
- **Konyo** (to help) → **Konyore** (to help each other)
- **Hera** (to love) → **Herore** (to love each other)

Usage:
- **Gin goyore** = They are hitting each other
- **Wan wakonyore** = We help each other
- **Ji herore** = People love each other

---

**Emphasis and Intensifiers:**

**Using "ahinya" (very/really):**
- **Ber ahinya** = Very good
- **Duong' ahinya** = Very big
- **Tek ahinya** = Very hard

**Using "moloyo" (more than/most):**
- **Ber moloyo** = Better / Most good
- **Duong' moloyo** = Bigger / Biggest
- **Tek moloyo** = Harder / Hardest

**Using "mana" (only/just):**
- **An mana** = Only me / Just me
- **Chieng' mana** = Just today
- **Gimoro mana** = Just something

**Using "kende" (only/alone):**
- **An kende** = Me alone / Only me
- **Achiel kende** = Only one
- **Nyathi kende** = Only the child

---

**Complex Sentence Structures:**

**Multiple Clauses:**
- **Ka ibiro kendo ka in gi pesa, to wabiro dhi ohala** = If you come and if you have money, then we will go to the market

**Embedded Questions:**
- **Ok ang'eyo ni en dhi kanye** = I don't know where he/she went
- **Penja ni en timo ang'o** = Ask him/her what he/she is doing

**Temporal Clauses:**
- **Kane achiemo, en nobiro** = When I was eating, he/she came
- **Bang' chiemo, wabiro dhi** = After eating, we will go
- **Kapok achiemo, ok anyal dhi** = Before I eat, I cannot go

---

**Idiomatic Verb Constructions:**

**"Bedo gi" (to have):**
- **An bedo gi pesa** = I have money
- **En bedo gi jamni** = He/She has animals

**"Dwaro" (to want) + infinitive:**
- **Adwaro dhi** = I want to go
- **En odwaro chiemo** = He/She wants to eat

**"Nyalo" (can/able) + infinitive:**
- **Anyalo timo** = I can do
- **Ok onyalo biro** = He/She cannot come

**"Kare" (should/supposed to):**
- **Kare adhi** = I should go
- **Kare otimo** = He/She should do
