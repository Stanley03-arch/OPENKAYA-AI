**Luo Proverbs and Sayings (Ngeche mag Jaluo)**

**Proverbs About Wisdom and Knowledge:**

1. **Ng'at ma ok owinjo wach, winjo mana ywak.**
   - Translation: A person who doesn't listen to advice will only hear crying.
   - Meaning: Those who don't take advice will face consequences.

2. **Rieko ok nyal pongʼ e wich ng'ato achiel.**
   - Translation: Wisdom cannot fill one person's head alone.
   - Meaning: No one knows everything; we need each other's knowledge.

3. **Ng'at ma ok osomo, chalo gi ng'at muofu.**
   - Translation: A person who hasn't studied is like a blind person.
   - Meaning: Education opens our eyes to the world.

4. **Puonj nyathi yoreni to ok ipuonje ywak.**
   - Translation: Teach a child the way but don't teach them to cry.
   - Meaning: Guide children but let them learn from experience.

5. **Ng'at ma ok ng'eyo, ok wil.**
   - Translation: A person who doesn't know is not foolish.
   - Meaning: Ignorance is not stupidity; everyone can learn.

**Proverbs About Unity and Community:**

6. **Lwet achiel ok nyal kwano wich.**
   - Translation: One hand cannot wash the head.
   - Meaning: We need cooperation and teamwork.

7. **Oganda achiel ok nyal loso piny.**
   - Translation: One community alone cannot build a nation.
   - Meaning: Unity of different groups is essential.

8. **Ji duto gin achiel e wang' Nyasaye.**
   - Translation: All people are one in God's eyes.
   - Meaning: We are all equal before God.

9. **Ng'at ma ok dwar osiepene, ok nyal bedo gi osiep.**
   - Translation: A person who doesn't want friends cannot have friends.
   - Meaning: Friendship requires effort and willingness.

10. **Kanyakla achiel ok kel oro.**
    - Translation: One finger cannot pick up a grain.
    - Meaning: Cooperation is necessary for success.

**Proverbs About Patience and Time:**

11. **Kinde duto nigi thuolone.**
    - Translation: Every time has its snake.
    - Meaning: Every period has its challenges.

12. **Ng'at ma rito, chiembe ok rum.**
    - Translation: A person who waits, their food doesn't get burnt.
    - Meaning: Patience brings good results.

13. **Chieng' ok rum e kor ng'ato.**
    - Translation: The sun doesn't set in someone's lap.
    - Meaning: Time waits for no one.

14. **Kinde ma ok onego itim gimoro, en kinde ma onego ibed maber.**
    - Translation: Time when you shouldn't do something is time you should stay well.
    - Meaning: Sometimes doing nothing is the right action.

**Proverbs About Hard Work:**

15. **Ng'at ma ok pur, ok cham.**
    - Translation: A person who doesn't farm doesn't eat.
    - Meaning: Hard work is necessary for survival.

16. **Tich matek kelo pesa.**
    - Translation: Hard work brings money.
    - Meaning: Effort leads to reward.

17. **Ng'at ma nindo e odiechieng', ok nyal yudo gimoro.**
    - Translation: A person who sleeps during the day cannot find anything.
    - Meaning: Laziness leads to poverty.

18. **Tich ok negi ng'ato.**
    - Translation: Work doesn't kill anyone.
    - Meaning: Hard work is beneficial, not harmful.

**Proverbs About Family:**

19. **Dala ok dhi maonge ng'ato.**
    - Translation: Home doesn't go anywhere without someone.
    - Meaning: Family is always there for you.

20. **Min nyathi en min piny.**
    - Translation: A child's mother is the mother of the world.
    - Meaning: Mothers are universally important.

21. **Nyathi ma ok ineno wangʼe, ok inyal rite.**
    - Translation: A child whose eyes you don't see, you cannot care for.
    - Meaning: You must be present to care for loved ones.

22. **Wuoro gi min gin tunge mar ot.**
    - Translation: Father and mother are the pillars of the house.
    - Meaning: Parents are the foundation of the family.

**Proverbs About Character and Behavior:**

23. **Ng'at ma wuoyo mangʼeny, timo matin.**
    - Translation: A person who talks a lot does little.
    - Meaning: Actions speak louder than words.

24. **Ng'at ma rach, ok nyal pand rachne.**
    - Translation: A bad person cannot hide their badness.
    - Meaning: True character always shows.

25. **Ng'at maber, ji ng'eyo.**
    - Translation: A good person, people know.
    - Meaning: Good character is recognized by all.

26. **Wuon kisumu ok nyal pando.**
    - Translation: The owner of shame cannot hide.
    - Meaning: Shameful acts will be discovered.

27. **Ng'at ma ok luoro, ok nyal bedo gi luor.**
    - Translation: A person who doesn't respect cannot have respect.
    - Meaning: Respect is reciprocal.

**Proverbs About Truth and Honesty:**

28. **Adier ok nyal pand.**
    - Translation: Truth cannot hide.
    - Meaning: The truth always comes out.

29. **Miriambo ok nyal loso ot.**
    - Translation: Maybe cannot build a house.
    - Meaning: Certainty and commitment are needed for success.

30. **Ng'at ma wacho adier, ok nyal wil.**
    - Translation: A person who speaks truth cannot be foolish.
    - Meaning: Honesty is wisdom.

**Proverbs About Life and Death:**

31. **Tho ok nyal ng'eyo ng'ato.**
    - Translation: Death doesn't know anyone.
    - Meaning: Death comes to everyone regardless of status.

32. **Ng'ima en mana wuoth.**
    - Translation: Life is just a journey.
    - Meaning: Life is temporary and we're all passing through.

33. **Chieng' ma ibiro thoe, ok ing'eyo.**
    - Translation: The day you will die, you don't know.
    - Meaning: Life is uncertain; live well today.

**Proverbs About Food and Hospitality:**

34. **Chiemo ma ok ipog, ok nyal cham.**
    - Translation: Food that you don't share cannot be eaten.
    - Meaning: Sharing brings joy and community.

35. **Wendo ok nyal chiemo kende.**
    - Translation: A visitor cannot eat alone.
    - Meaning: Hospitality requires sharing with guests.

36. **Ng'at ma ok chiwo, ok nyal yudo.**
    - Translation: A person who doesn't give cannot receive.
    - Meaning: Generosity begets generosity.

**Proverbs About Respect:**

37. **Luor mar jaduong' en mwandu.**
    - Translation: Respect for elders is wealth.
    - Meaning: Respecting elders brings blessings.

38. **Ng'at ma ok luoro jodongo, ok nyal dongo maber.**
    - Translation: A person who doesn't respect elders cannot grow well.
    - Meaning: Respect for elders is essential for proper development.

**Common Sayings:**

39. **Gima osetimore, ok nyal dok chien.**
    - What has been done cannot return back.
    - Meaning: You can't undo the past.

40. **Wach moro ok yal wach moro.**
    - One word is not like another word.
    - Meaning: Words have different meanings and impacts.

41. **Ng'at ma ok dwar kony, ok nyal konyi.**
    - A person who doesn't want help, you cannot help them.
    - Meaning: People must be willing to accept help.

42. **Kinde duto nigi thuolone to bende nigi yathne.**
    - Every time has its snake but also its medicine.
    - Meaning: Every problem has a solution.

43. **Ng'at ma biro, ok nyal gengo.**
    - A person who is coming cannot be prevented.
    - Meaning: What is meant to happen will happen.

44. **Piny ok rum.**
    - The world doesn't end.
    - Meaning: Life goes on; there's always hope.

45. **Ng'at ma ok nyal wuoyo, ok nyal yudo.**
    - A person who cannot speak cannot receive.
    - Meaning: You must ask for what you need.
