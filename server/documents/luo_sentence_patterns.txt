**Luo Sentence Patterns & Construction (Chenro mar Weche e Dholuo)**

**Basic Sentence Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Subject + Verb**
- **An adhi** = I go
- **In ibiro** = You come
- **En ochiemo** = He/She eats
- **Wan watimo** = We do
- **Gin giwuotho** = They walk

**Pattern 2: Subject + Verb + Object**
- **An achiemo kuon** = I eat ugali
- **In isomo buku** = You read a book
- **En otedo chiemo** = He/She cooks food
- **Wan wang'iewo jamni** = We buy animals
- **Gin gidwaro pesa** = They want money

**Pattern 3: Subject + Verb + Adjective**
- **Ot duong'** = The house is big
- **Chiemo ber** = The food is good
- **Piny liet** = The weather is hot
- **Buku tek** = The book is difficult
- **Tich yot** = The work is easy

**Pattern 4: Subject + "gi" (have) + Object**
- **An gi pesa** = I have money
- **In gi simu** = You have a phone
- **En gi joode** = He/She has family
- **Wan gi jamni** = We have animals
- **Gin gi ot** = They have a house

**Pattern 5: Subject + Verb + Location**
- **An adak Kisumu** = I live in Kisumu
- **In idhi skul** = You go to school
- **En obedo e ot** = He/She is at home
- **Wan wadhi ohala** = We go to the market
- **Gin gitimo tich e ofis** = They work in the office

---

**Negation Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Ok + Subject + Verb**
- **Ok adhi** = I don't go
- **Ok ibiro** = You don't come
- **Ok ochiemo** = He/She doesn't eat
- **Ok watimo** = We don't do
- **Ok giwuotho** = They don't walk

**Pattern 2: Subject + ok + gi + Object**
- **An ok gi pesa** = I don't have money
- **In ok gi simu** = You don't have a phone
- **En ok gi joode** = He/She doesn't have family

**Pattern 3: Ok + Adjective**
- **Ok ber** = Not good
- **Ok duong'** = Not big
- **Ok tek** = Not difficult

---

**Question Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Question Word + Subject + Verb**
- **Ang'o itimo?** = What are you doing?
- **Kanye idhi?** = Where are you going?
- **Sa adi ibiro?** = What time are you coming?
- **Ng'a owacho?** = Who said?
- **Kare ang'o?** = Why?

**Pattern 2: Subject + Verb + Question Marker**
- **In idhi?** = Are you going?
- **En obiro?** = Is he/she coming?
- **Wan watimo?** = Are we doing?
- **Gin gichiemo?** = Are they eating?

**Pattern 3: Verb + gi + Question Word**
- **In gi ang'o?** = What do you have?
- **En gi ng'a?** = Who does he/she have?
- **Wan gi adi?** = How many do we have?

---

**Compound Sentence Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Sentence + kendo + Sentence (and)**
- **Adhi tich kendo abiro dok odhiambo** = I go to work and I will return in the evening
- **En ochiemo kendo ometho** = He/She eats and drinks
- **Wan wasomo kendo watimo tich** = We study and we work

**Pattern 2: Sentence + to + Sentence (but)**
- **Adwaro dhi, to aonge gi pesa** = I want to go, but I don't have money
- **En ber, to tek** = It's good, but difficult
- **Wan wadwaro konyo, to ok wanyal** = We want to help, but we cannot

**Pattern 3: Ka + Sentence, Sentence (if/when)**
- **Ka koth ochue, ok wabiro dhi** = If it rains, we won't go
- **Ka ibiro, wabiro chiemo** = When you come, we will eat
- **Ka in gi pesa, ng'iew gimoro** = If you have money, buy something

**Pattern 4: Nikech + Sentence (because)**
- **Ok adhi nikech atuo** = I'm not going because I'm sick
- **Amor nikech ayudo tich** = I'm happy because I got a job
- **Gidhi nikech gidwaro** = They go because they want to

---

**Time-Related Patterns:**

**Present Continuous:**
- **An a + verb + -o** = I am (verb)ing
- **An achiemo** = I am eating
- **An awuotho** = I am walking
- **An atimo** = I am doing

**Past Tense:**
- **Ne + subject + verb**
- **Ne adhi** = I went
- **Ne ochiemo** = He/She ate
- **Ne watimo** = We did

**Future Tense:**
- **Subject + biro + verb**
- **Abiro dhi** = I will go
- **Obiro chiemo** = He/She will eat
- **Wabiro timo** = We will do

**Present Perfect:**
- **Subject + ose + verb**
- **Asedhi** = I have gone
- **Osechiemo** = He/She has eaten
- **Wasetimo** = We have done

---

**Possession Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Noun + mar + Owner**
- **Buku mar Otieno** = Otieno's book
- **Ot mar wuora** = My father's house
- **Simu mar min** = Mother's phone
- **Gari mar jatelo** = The boss's car

**Pattern 2: Owner + -a/e + Noun**
- **Bukuna** = Your book
- **Ota** = My house
- **Simune** = His/Her phone
- **Garwa** = Our car

---

**Comparison Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Adjective + moloyo**
- **Ber moloyo** = Better / More good
- **Duong' moloyo** = Bigger
- **Tek moloyo** = Harder / More difficult
- **Yot moloyo** = Easier

**Pattern 2: Subject + Adjective + moloyo + Object**
- **Mani ber moloyo mano** = This is better than that
- **Otieno duong' moloyo Omondi** = Otieno is bigger than Omondi
- **Buku mani tek moloyo mano** = This book is harder than that

**Pattern 3: Chalo gi (like/similar to)**
- **En chalo gi wuon-gi** = He is like his father
- **Gimani chalo gi mano** = This is like that
- **Piny kawuono chalo gi nyoro** = Today's weather is like yesterday's

---

**Causative Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Verb + -bo (make someone do)**
- **Chiemo** → **Chiembo** (to feed)
- **Nindo** → **Nindbo** (to put to sleep)
- **Somo** → **Sombo** (to teach/make read)

Usage:
- **Min chiembo nyathi** = Mother feeds the child
- **Wuoro nindbo nyathi** = Father puts the child to sleep
- **Japuonj sombo nyithindo** = Teacher teaches children

---

**Reciprocal Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Verb + -ore (each other)**
- **Goyo** → **Goyore** (hit each other)
- **Konyo** → **Konyore** (help each other)
- **Hera** → **Herore** (love each other)

Usage:
- **Gin goyore** = They hit each other
- **Wan wakonyore** = We help each other
- **Ji herore** = People love each other

---

**Emphasis Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Using "mana" (only/just)**
- **An mana** = Only me
- **Chieng' mana** = Just today
- **Gimoro mana** = Just something

**Pattern 2: Using "kende" (alone/only)**
- **An kende** = Me alone
- **Achiel kende** = Only one
- **En kende** = He/She alone

**Pattern 3: Using "ahinya" (very/really)**
- **Ber ahinya** = Very good
- **Duong' ahinya** = Very big
- **Tek ahinya** = Very hard

---

**Polite Request Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Kiyie + verb (please)**
- **Kiyie ikonya** = Please help me
- **Kiyie ikelona pi** = Please bring me water
- **Kiyie ibiro** = Please come

**Pattern 2: Anyalo + verb? (May I...?)**
- **Anyalo dhi?** = May I go?
- **Anyalo ng'iewo?** = May I buy?
- **Anyalo penjo?** = May I ask?

**Pattern 3: Inyalo + verb? (Can you...?)**
- **Inyalo konya?** = Can you help me?
- **Inyalo nyisa?** = Can you show me?
- **Inyalo rito?** = Can you wait?

---

**Location Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: E + Location (at/in)**
- **E ot** = At home
- **E skul** = At school
- **E ohala** = At the market
- **E Kisumu** = In Kisumu

**Pattern 2: Ir + Person (at someone's place)**
- **Ir wuora** = At father's place
- **Ir jadak** = At the doctor's
- **Ir osiepena** = At my friend's place

**Pattern 3: Kanye (where)**
- **Idak kanye?** = Where do you live?
- **Idhi kanye?** = Where are you going?
- **En kanye?** = Where is he/she?

---

**Purpose Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Mondo + verb (in order to)**
- **Adhi skul mondo asom** = I go to school in order to study
- **Atimo tich mondo ayud pesa** = I work in order to get money
- **Achiemo mondo abed motegno** = I eat in order to be strong

**Pattern 2: Ne + purpose**
- **Abiro ne konyi** = I will come to help you
- **Adhi ne ng'iewo** = I go to buy
- **Obiro ne neno** = He/She comes to see

---

**Conditional Patterns (Advanced):**

**Real Condition (Ka + present):**
- **Ka ibiro, wabiro chiemo** = If you come, we will eat
- **Ka koth ochue, ok wadhi** = If it rains, we won't go
- **Ka in gi pesa, ng'iew** = If you have money, buy

**Unreal Condition (Ka ne + past):**
- **Ka ne an gi pesa, da ng'iewo** = If I had money, I would buy
- **Ka ne en ni ka, da okonyo** = If he/she were here, he/she would help
- **Ka ne wang'eyo, da wawacho** = If we knew, we would tell

**Past Unreal (Ka ne ose + verb):**
- **Ka ne osechiemo, ok da otuo** = If he/she had eaten, he/she wouldn't be sick
- **Ka ne isomo, da iloso** = If you had studied, you would have passed

---

**Reporting Patterns:**

**Direct Speech:**
- **En owacho, "Adhi dala"** = He/She said, "I'm going home"

**Indirect Speech:**
- **En owacho ni odhi dala** = He/She said that he/she is going home
- **Omondi owacho ni ochiemo** = Omondi said that he is eating
- **Ne owacho ni obiro** = He/She said that he/she will come

---

**Descriptive Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Noun + ma + Description**
- **Ng'at ma ber** = A good person
- **Ot ma duong'** = A big house
- **Chiemo ma mit** = Delicious food

**Pattern 2: Noun + ma + nigi + Quality**
- **Ng'at ma nigi chuny maber** = A person who has a good heart
- **Ot ma nigi rude** = A house that has rooms
- **Buku ma nigi weche mathoth** = A book that has many things

---

**Sequential Action Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: First, then (Mokwongo, eka)**
- **Mokwongo chiemo, eka dhi** = First eat, then go
- **Mokwongo somo, eka timo tich** = First study, then work

**Pattern 2: After (Bang')**
- **Bang' chiemo, wabiro dhi** = After eating, we will go
- **Bang' tich, abiro biro** = After work, I will come

**Pattern 3: Before (Kapok)**
- **Kapok adhi, nyaka achiemo** = Before I go, I must eat
- **Kapok itimo, par** = Before you do, think
