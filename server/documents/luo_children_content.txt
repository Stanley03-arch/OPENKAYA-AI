**Luo Children's Content (Weche mag Nyithindo e Dholuo)**

**Children's Stories (Sigendni mag Nyithindo):**

**Story 1: Nyathi gi Gueno (The Child and the Chicken)**

Ne nitie nyathi matin ma nyinge Auma. Auma ne nigi gueno matin ma ne ohero ahinya. Ne oluongo gueno ni "Akelo."

Okinyi ka okinyi, Auma ne chiembo Akelo. Ne omiye ngano kendo pi. Akelo ne dongo kendo obedo maduong'.

Chieng' achiel, Akelo ne onywolo nyithinde. Ne onywolo nyithindo apar! Auma ne omor ahinya.

Koro Auma nigi gueno apar gachiel. En gi Akelo kod nyithinde apar.

**Moral:** Ka irito gimoro maber, obiro medore.
(If you take care of something well, it will multiply.)

---

**Story 2: Otieno ma ne Ohero Somo (Otieno Who Loved to Study)**

Otieno ne en nyathi ma hera somo. Ne osomo okinyi, odiechieng', kendo otieno.

Osiepene ne goye siboi. Ne giwacho: "Otieno, bi wang'o! Kik isom pile!"

To Otieno ne ok odewo. Ne osiko kosomo. Ne osomo Kiswahili, English, Hisabati, kendo Sayansi.

Kane exam obiro, Otieno ne oloso maber ahinya. Ne obedo ng'at mokwongo e klasi.

Koro osiepene duto dwaro somo kaka Otieno.

**Moral:** Somo kelo loch. (Study brings success.)

---

**Story 3: Anyango ma ne Konyo Min (Anyango Who Helped Mother)**

Anyango ne en nyako matin ma nigi chuny maber. Pile ne okonyo min e ot.

Okinyi, ne okonyo yuago ot. Odiechieng', ne okonyo tedo chiemo. Odhiambo, ne okonyo luoko gige.

Min ne omor gi Anyango. Ne owacho: "Anyango, in nyathina maber. Nyasaye biro gwedhi."

Higni mathoth nokalo. Anyango nodongo kendo obedo dhako maber. Ne nigi joode kendo ne gidak gi mor.

**Moral:** Ng'at ma konyo joode yudo gweth. (A person who helps their family receives blessings.)

---

**Story 4: Omondi gi Guok (Omondi and the Dog)**

Omondi ne oyudo guok matin e yo. Guok ne ool kendo ne nigi kech. Omondi ne okawo guok kendo otere dala.

Ne ochiembo guok kendo omiye pi. Guok ne dongo kendo obedo osiep mar Omondi.

Chieng' achiel, thuol maduong' nobiro e ot. Guok ne oringo kendo ogoyo thuol. Thuol ne oringo odhi.

Omondi ne omor. Guok ne oreso ngimane.

**Moral:** Ber miyo ber. (Goodness returns goodness.)

---

**Story 5: Akinyi ma ne Dwaro Huodho (Akinyi Who Wanted to Fly)**

Akinyi ne en nyako matin ma ne dwaro huodho kaka winyo. Ne oparo: "Dabed maber ka danyalo huodho!"

Chieng' achiel, ne otemo mako bwombwe mag winyo. To bwombwe ne opodho. Ne ok onyal huodho.

Min ne owachone: "Anyango, ji ok nyal huodho. To inyalo somo kendo bedo jopuonj mar winyo."

Akinyi ne osomo matek. Nodongo kendo obedo japuonj mar winyo. Ne opuonjo ji kuom winyo duto.

**Moral:** Kata ka ok inyalo timo gimoro, inyalo somo kuome. (Even if you can't do something, you can learn about it.)

**Children's Rhymes (Wende mag Nyithindo):**

**Rhyme 1: Kwan Nyaka Apar (Count to Ten)**

Achiel, ariyo, adek, ang'wen
(One, two, three, four)
Abich, auchiel, abiriyo, aboro
(Five, six, seven, eight)
Ochiko, apar - waseloso!
(Nine, ten - we're done!)

---

**Rhyme 2: Gueno Mara (My Chicken)**

Gueno mara, gueno mara
(My chicken, my chicken)
Ber moloyo gueno duto
(Better than all chickens)
Chiemo ngano, chiemo ngano
(Eats grain, eats grain)
Kendo nywolo nyithindo
(And lays eggs)

---

**Rhyme 3: Chieng' Rieny (The Sun Shines)**

Chieng' rieny, chieng' rieny
(The sun shines, the sun shines)
Piny ler, piny ler
(The world is bright, the world is bright)
Wan wang'o, wan wang'o
(We play, we play)
Wan mor, wan mor!
(We're happy, we're happy!)

---

**Rhyme 4: Dala Mara (My Home)**

Dala mara, dala mara
(My home, my home)
Kama ahero ahinya
(The place I love so much)
Wuora ni kanyo, mina ni kanyo
(Father is there, mother is there)
Kendo owetena duto
(And all my siblings)

---

**Rhyme 5: Rech e Nam (Fish in the Lake)**

Rech e nam, rech e nam
(Fish in the lake, fish in the lake)
Mathoth moloyo
(So very many)
Jachien mako, jachien mako
(Fisherman catches, fisherman catches)
Kendo wachiemo!
(And we eat!)

**Children's Games (Tuche mag Nyithindo):**

**1. Bao**
Traditional board game played with seeds or stones. Teaches counting and strategy.

**2. Ajua**
A game where children form circles and sing while one child dances in the middle.

**3. Ringo gi Mako (Run and Catch)**
Tag game where one child chases others.

**4. Pando Kidi (Throwing Stones)**
Game of accuracy throwing stones at targets.

**5. Goyo Mpira (Playing Ball)**
Football/soccer - most popular children's game.

**Simple Questions Children Ask:**

Q: **Mani en ang'o?** (What is this?)
A: **Mani en buku.** (This is a book.)

Q: **Rangi mare en ang'o?** (What color is it?)
A: **Rangi mare rachar.** (Its color is red.)

Q: **Adwaro chiemo** (I want food)
A: **Chiemo biro biro.** (Food is coming.)

Q: **Anyalo dhi wang'o?** (Can I go play?)
A: **Ee, inyalo dhi.** (Yes, you can go.)

Q: **Mani en mar ng'a?** (Whose is this?)
A: **Mani en mara.** (This is mine.)
