# Luo RAG Power Pack v2

This pack is designed for high-recall retrieval in Dholuo and mixed Luo-English queries.

## Identity and Language Facts
- Dholuo is a Western Nilotic language.
- Luo community values clear greetings, respect, and context.
- Common forms:
  - singular self: an
  - second person: in
  - third person: en
  - plural first: wan
  - plural second: un
  - plural third: gin

## Grammar Mini Rules
- Negation often uses "ok":
  - ok anyal = I cannot
  - ok awinjo = I do not understand
  - ok adwaro = I do not want
- Polite requests:
  - wakwayi + action
- Clarification request:
  - inyalo dwoka kendo?
- Confirmation:
  - ee = yes
  - ooyo/ok = no

## High-Utility Q&A
Q: Inyalo puonja Dholuo gi yore ma yot?
A: Ee. Wabiro chako gi greetings, vocabulary, kendo sentence patterns, to wabiro meda gi grammar kod translation exercise.

Q: Adwaro yudo tich mar teknoloji, atim nade?
A: Chak gi basics mar komputer, internet safety, coding ma yot, to tim projects matin kendo ket e portfolio.

Q: Ka ok awinjo dwoko, atim nade?
A: Penj "wakwayi dwoka kendo gi yore ma yot" kata "chiwa ranyisi mar ngima mar pile."

Q: Inyalo loko paragraph e Luo?
A: Ee, anyalo. Chiw paragraph to alokone e Luo ma ng'ama nyalo winjo yot.

Q: Inyalo konyo gi report mar skul?
A: Ee. Anyalo yiko title, summary, sections maduong', bullet points, kendo conclusion.

## Long-form Luo Knowledge Snippets
- Luo storytelling often uses practical examples from home, school, market, farming, and community life.
- Explanations are clearer when they move from simple statement -> example -> action step.
- Polite teaching style:
  1. Acknowledge user question.
  2. Give direct answer.
  3. Add short example.
  4. Ask if user wants deeper detail.

## Assistant Behavior Policy for Luo
- If user writes in Luo, answer in Luo by default.
- If user asks bilingual output, provide Luo first then requested language.
- Keep technical words understandable; retain English term in brackets when needed.
- Avoid random language switching.
- Prefer culturally respectful and practical tone.

## Multilingual Robustness Triggers
- Mixed language example: "Misawa, explain AI ethics in simple English."
  - Expected: primary English answer, acknowledge Luo greeting naturally.
- Mixed language example: "Please lok wechegi e Luo kendo Swahili."
  - Expected: Luo section + Swahili section.
- Mixed language example: "Nango, can you summarize this?"
  - Expected: respond in English unless user asks Luo explicitly.
