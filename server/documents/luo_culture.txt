**Luo Cultural Knowledge and Traditions (Timbe gi Chike mag Jaluo)**

**Traditional Ceremonies (Timbegi Machon):**

**1. Tero Buru (Funeral Rites):**
- **Tero buru** en nyasi mar liel maduong' e kind Jaluo. (Tero buru is an important funeral rite among the Luo).
- Ka ng'ato otho, joode luongo oganda duto mondo gibi rito liel. (When someone dies, the family calls the whole community to attend the funeral).
- Liel nyalo kawo ndalo mathoth, kinde moko hadi juma achiel. (Funerals can take many days, sometimes up to one week).
- Ji goyo bul, wer, kendo gichiemo kaachiel. (People play drums, sing, and eat together).

**2. Nyombo (Widowhood Cleansing):**
- **Nyombo** en chik machon ma Jaluo moko pod luwo. (Nyombo is an old custom that some Luo still follow).
- Chikni itimo bang' tho mar chwor. (This custom is done after the death of a husband).

**3. Naming Ceremonies (Nying Nyithindo):**
- Jaluo chiwo nyithindo nying kaluwore gi kinde ma onyuolgi. (Luo give children names according to the time they were born).
- **Onyango/Anyango** - Onyuol okinyi godhiambo (Born in the morning).
- **Otieno/Atieno** - Onyuol otieno (Born at night).
- **Ochieng'/Achieng'** - Onyuol ka chieng' rieny (Born when the sun is bright).
- **Okoth/Akoth** - Onyuol ka koth chue (Born during rain).
- **Omondi/Amondi** - Onyuol okinyi gokinyi (Born early morning).
- **Ouma/Auma** - Onyuol kar chiro (Born at the hospital/health center).
- **Adhiambo** - Onyuol odhiambo (Born in the evening).
- **Awino** - Onyuol ka ji nindo (Born when people are asleep).

**4. Marriage Customs (Chike mag Kend):**
- **Ayie** en chik mar kend e kind Jaluo. (Ayie is the marriage custom among the Luo).
- Wuoyi gi joode kelo pesa gi jamni ne jood nyako. (The man and his family bring money and animals to the girl's family).
- Jamni mag ayie nyalo bedo dhok, diek, kata pesa. (Marriage gifts can be cows, goats, or money).
- Bang' ayie, nyako dhi dala chwore. (After ayie, the girl goes to the husband's home).

**Traditional Music and Dance (Thum gi Miel):**

**1. Ohangla:**
- **Ohangla** en thum maduong' mar Jaluo. (Ohangla is a major Luo music genre).
- Gigoyo bul maluong ni **Ohangla** gi **Oporo**. (They play drums called Ohangla and Oporo).
- Thum ohangla mamit ahinya kendo jomiel miel gi teko. (Ohangla music is very sweet and dancers dance with energy).

**2. Benga:**
- **Benga** en thum manyien ma Jaluo oloso. (Benga is a modern music that the Luo created).
- Benga riwore gi gitar gi bul. (Benga combines guitar and drums).

**3. Traditional Instruments (Gige Thum Machon):**
- **Nyatiti** - Gitar machon mar Jaluo (Traditional Luo lyre).
- **Bul** - Drum (Drum).
- **Oporo** - Bul maduong' (Large drum).
- **Orutu** - Gige thum ma igoyo gi tol (Single-stringed fiddle).
- **Asili** - Shaker/Rattle (Shaker).

**Sacred Places (Kuonde Maler):**

**1. Kit Mikayi:**
- **Kit Mikayi** en lwanda maduong' man Seme, Kisumu. (Kit Mikayi is a large rock formation in Seme, Kisumu).
- Nyinge en "Kidi mar Chi Mokwongo" (Its name is "Stone of the First Wife").
- Ji dhi Kit Mikayi mondo gilam kendo gikwa gik moko. (People go to Kit Mikayi to pray and ask for things).
- En kama duong' ahinya ne Jaluo. (It is a very sacred place for the Luo).

**2. Got Ramogi:**
- **Got Ramogi** en got maduong' man Alego. (Got Ramogi is a large hill in Alego).
- Iluongo kamano nikech Ramogi Ajwang', kwaro mar Jaluo. (It's called that because of Ramogi Ajwang', the Luo patriarch).

**Traditional Food (Chiemo Machon):**

**1. Kuon gi Rech:**
- **Kuon gi rech** en chiemo mamit moloyo ne Jaluo. (Ugali and fish is the most favorite food for the Luo).
- Rech oa e Nam Lolwe. (Fish comes from Lake Victoria).
- Rech mamit moloyo gin **Mbuta** gi **Kamongo**. (The most delicious fish are Mbuta and Kamongo).

**2. Alode:**
- **Alode** en mboga ma Jaluo chiemo. (Alode is vegetables that Luo eat).
- Alode mamit gin **Apoth**, **Boo**, **Osuga**. (Delicious vegetables are Apoth, Boo, Osuga).

**3. Omena:**
- **Omena** en rech matindo ma ji chiemo gi kuon. (Omena is small fish that people eat with ugali).

**Traditional Leadership (Telo Machon):**

**1. Ruoth:**
- **Ruoth** en jatelo maduong' mar oganda. (Ruoth is the chief leader of the community).
- Ruoth rito chike kod timbe mag oganda. (The Ruoth maintains customs and traditions of the community).

**2. Jaduong' Gweng':**
- **Jaduong' gweng'** en jatelo mar gweng'. (Jaduong' gweng' is the village elder).
- Gin jiwo ji kendo gitieko larruok. (They advise people and resolve conflicts).

**3. Jodongo:**
- **Jodongo** gin ji machon ma nigi rieko. (Jodongo are old people who have wisdom).
- Ji luongo jodongo mondo gipuonj timbe machon. (People call elders to teach old customs).

**Spiritual Beliefs (Yie mar Roho):**

**1. Nyasaye (Were):**
- **Nyasaye** kata **Were** en Jachwe. (Nyasaye or Were is the Creator/God).
- Jaluo yie ni Nyasaye ema nochweyo piny gi ji duto. (Luo believe that Nyasaye created the world and all people).

**2. Juogi (Ancestral Spirits):**
- **Juogi** gin chuny jomoko mosethone. (Juogi are spirits of the dead).
- Jaluo yie ni juogi rito joodgi. (Luo believe that ancestral spirits protect their families).

**3. Ajuoga (Medicine Men/Healers):**
- **Ajuoga** gin ji ma nigi teko mar thiedho ji. (Ajuoga are people who have power to heal people).
- Gin tiyo gi yath machon. (They use traditional medicine).

**Important Historical Figures (Ji Maduong' e History):**

**1. Ramogi Ajwang':**
- **Ramogi Ajwang'** e kwaro maduong' mar Jaluo. (Ramogi Ajwang' is the great patriarch of the Luo).
- En ema nokelo Jaluo e piny Kenya. (He is the one who brought the Luo to Kenya).

**2. Oginga Odinga:**
- **Oginga Odinga** ne en ker mokwongo mar Kenya. (Oginga Odinga was the first Vice President of Kenya).
- Ne en jabura maduong' e siasa mar Kenya. (He was a major politician in Kenyan politics).
- Nyinge kamano ne en "Jaramogi" - tiende ni "Wuod Ramogi". (His title was "Jaramogi" - meaning "Son of Ramogi").

**3. Raila Odinga:**
- **Raila Odinga** en wuod Oginga Odinga. (Raila Odinga is the son of Oginga Odinga).
- En jabura maduong' e siasa mar Kenya kawuono. (He is a major politician in Kenyan politics today).
- Nyinge kamano en "Agwambo" - tiende ni "Mysterious One". (His nickname is "Agwambo" - meaning "Mysterious One").

**4. Tom Mboya:**
- **Tom Mboya** ne en jabura maduong' e siasa mar Kenya. (Tom Mboya was a major politician in Kenyan politics).
- Ne en jatelo mar trade unions. (He was a leader of trade unions).

**Traditional Games (Tich Miel):**
- **Ajua** - Bao game (Traditional board game).
- **Ringo** - Running races (Running races).
- **Goyo pilo** - Playing football (Playing football).
- **Miel gi tol** - Rope jumping (Rope jumping).
